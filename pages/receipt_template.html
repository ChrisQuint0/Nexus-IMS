General Guidelines:
  1. The gadget/equipment issued to you is only borrowed from the City Government of Pasig and will be returned once you leave Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Pasig.
  2. In case of Loss, Theft, or Damage of the borrowed gadget, it is your duty and diligence to the device lent. Notify the bearer immediately not later than twenty (20) days after the incident happened to the custodian of the "Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Pasig" of such fact. Provided that, if the gadget lost is willfully or negligently lost, the applicable surcharge shall be imposed against me, in the amount of replacement, to replace the property with the same specifications as the device lent and without prejudice to another sanction which the City of Pasig may further impose against me. In case of damage, I shall be liable for the item repaired at my own expense to bring it back to working condition. However, in case it cannot be repaired, or the cost is excessive, I shall undertake to pay the actual price and surcharge.
  3. For cases attributed to Loss or Damage due to unavoidable circumstances, notify immediately the class adviser and MIS Personnel. Please prepare the following documents:
    1. Affidavit of Loss or Damage
    2. Affidavit of two disinterested persons who have personal knowledge of such fact/loss
    3. Additional documents for the following incident/circumstances: In the case of the following incident/occurrences:
      1. FIRE
        1. Progress and/or final report of the local Police/Fire Department or Station on the incident.
        2. List or Inventory of burned or destroyed properties.
        3. Authenticated picture(s) showing the city office or government properties razed by the fire.
      2. THEFT OR ROBBERY
        1. Progress and/or Final Police report on the theft or robbery case.
        2. Authenticated picture(s) taken relative to the robbery or theft incident.
      3. FORCE MAJEURE (EARTHQUAKE, TYPHOONS, AND OTHER NATURAL CALAMITIES)
        1. Detailed list/inventory of damaged government property/ies and those property/ies destroyed after the calamity.
        2. Certification issued by the official local weather bureau (PAGASA) or other similar government agency on the actual occurrence of the calamity.

In sending your concern via email, please include a video or screenshot of the problem so that we can initially assess it and make necessary recommendations to remedy the problem.
